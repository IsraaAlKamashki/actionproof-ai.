# ActionProof AI — demo video script

Target length: **2 minutes 35 seconds**. Record the screen with a clear voiceover. Keep the video public on YouTube.

## 0:00–0:20 — Problem

> Meetings generate plenty of notes, but not always clear execution. Owners and deadlines can be missing, risks emerge late, and an action may be called complete without proof. I built ActionProof AI to turn conversation into accountable, evidence-backed execution.

Show the Capture screen and the synthetic Atlas meeting notes.

## 0:20–0:55 — AI extraction

> ActionProof uses GPT-5.6 through the OpenAI Responses API. I paste meeting notes and run the analysis. The model returns structured commitments, but it is constrained to the source: it must identify missing information and quote the supporting text instead of inventing details.

Select **Load demo**, then **Analyze with ActionProof**.

## 0:55–1:30 — Control room

> The control room shows the complete execution picture: total commitments, risks, missing clarity, owners, deadlines, and the proof required for closure. Here, the finance baseline is blocking benefit validation, and the dashboard has no agreed owner. Every item links back to the source with a confidence score so the user remains in control.

Open one **View source** item, then close it.

## 1:30–1:58 — Evidence gate

> ActionProof does not treat a completion claim as completion. It defines the evidence standard and checks the submitted proof. When I provide a specific approved and signed outcome, the action becomes verified and the control-room metric updates.

Open **Verify evidence**, paste the prepared sample, and verify it.

Sample evidence:

> The approved vendor proposal was signed on 16 July and the approved finance baseline is attached for review.

## 1:58–2:20 — Executive brief

> Finally, ActionProof compresses the detail into an executive decision brief. It highlights what changed, where leadership attention is required, and the priority decision—without hiding uncertainty.

Select **Generate executive brief**.

## 2:20–2:35 — Codex and close

> I used Codex with GPT-5.6 to turn the idea into a working, responsive product: defining the workflow, building the interface and API route, testing the interactions, and deploying the result. ActionProof helps teams move from meeting notes to measurable, provable execution.

End on the executive brief and ActionProof name.
